    National Medical Data Management (NMDM) is the organized handling of healthcare data at a national level. It involves collecting, securely storing, and analyzing healthcare information from various sources. This data is standardized, enabling research, policy development, and improved patient care. It aids in public health monitoring, resource allocation, and healthcare innovation while prioritizing data security and privacy. During emergencies, it supports rapid response and resource allocation. In essence, it's crucial for informed decision-making, healthcare quality improvement, and public health management on a national scale.
    National data management involves the systematic organization, storage, maintenance, analysis, and utilization of data on a country-wide scale. It encompasses a broad range of information, often focusing on various sectors such as healthcare, education, finance, population statistics, environment, infrastructure, and more. The primary goal is to collect, process, and manage vast amounts of data to facilitate informed decision-making, policy formulation, research, and planning at the national level.
